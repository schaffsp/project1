Tofu and Tempeh

Historically, the most popular meat alternatives have been tofu and tempeh. They are both soy based and rich in protein, and have been eat for centuries in parts of Asia. Tofu is the older of the two and can be produced in soft and firm varieties. Tempeh is usually firmer and is often used in burger patties.

The process of making tofu, and tempeh by extension, is very similar to the process used to make cheese. Legend has it that the first batch of tofu was made by accident 2000 years ago when a careless farmer mixed sea salt and soy milk and left alone for a while. This is why Tofu's texture is very similar to many varieties of cheeses.
